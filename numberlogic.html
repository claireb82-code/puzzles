Nine

Classic 9×9 sudoku. Fill every row, column, and 3×3 box with 1–9, no repeats. Click a cell and type a digit (or use the keypad) — the row, column, and box light up to help you scan.

Rules

Every row, every column, and every 3×3 box must contain the digits 1 through 9, each exactly once.

Bold cells are given and can't be changed. Click any other cell to select it, then type 1–9 (or use the keypad). Backspace/Delete or the ✕ button clears a cell.

A red outline means that digit conflicts with another cell in its row, column, or box. Once a digit has been placed nine times it greys out on the keypad; clearing one of its cells brings it back. If every cell is filled but something's off, you'll get a message even without asking for a hint.
